Box breathing

Box breathing is a breathing technique that can be used to calm down during a stressful situation. By simply breathing in a rhythmic pattern, one's heartrate can decrease and return to normal.

Try using the animation below to practice box breathing! Whenever the box gets bigger, take a deep breath in. Hold that breath until the box starts to get smaller, and slowly exhale.

50 Activities

Let's be honest, being bored is never fun. Occasional boredom is perfectly healthy, and even should be embraced sometimes! However, if boredom gets difficult to tolerate and start to impact your mood, here's a list of fifty activities to do to occupy yourself!

(List was taken from Parade's article of a similar name)

  1. Learn a new skill. From card tricks to baking, the world is your oyster!
  2. Clean an area in your home. Start small, or take on a more daring room!
  3. Head out for a long walk. Find a nearby nature trail and take in the scenery!
  4. Learn a new language. Find out different ways to say the same greeting in different languages.
  5. Take some time to meditate. Or if you can't clear your thoughts, practice mindfulness.
  6. Write a song. Make it about someone important in your life as a way to say thank-you!
  7. Take a virtual (or physical!) trip to a museum. See the exhibits they have to offer.
  8. Try a new recipe or restaurant. Recipes are easily accessible online, and you might just find your new favorite lunch!
  9. Create a bucket list. You don't have to think way ahead, make a bucket list that you'd like to complete before summer starts!
  10. Enforce a new healthy habit. Plan to go to sleep earlier, or make your bed after getting up.
  11. Watch a fast-paced movie. Action movies keep you in the moment and engaged in the plot.
  12. Play your favorite video-game, or find a new one!
  13. Read or listen to a good book. Look through social media for certain genres, or catch up on a classic book you never got to read in school.
  14. Listen to music. Revisit an album you've been wanting to binge-listen to, or explore your favorite band's discography.
  15. Sky gaze. Lay a blanket outside and watch the clouds float by. What shapes or objects do you see?
  16. Buy an item you've been eyeing. If that phone charm is cute and your budget allows it, go right ahead and treat yourself! (Just don't overspend!)
  17. Text a friend. Catch up on the latest news, or plan a time to hang out.
  18. Start a journal. Journaling can be used for many things, whether it's to vent your feelings or to keep your events organized.
  19. Check out a store that just opened near you. Who knows? You might like what they sell, and they might even have discounts!
  20. Get some exercise in. Play a dance-workout video on YouTube and follow along, get your body moving!
  21. Write a letter to your future self. Ask them about what they'll have in the future, or where you are now. (Bonus points if you manage to send it to yourself in a few years.)
  22. Learn a new dance. Dances are prevalent in social media, why not learn a couple of them?
  23. Plant flowers. You can also start a veggie or herb garden to use in the kitchen.
  24. Make DIY posters. Or really just stick anything to your wall: printed photos, coloring book pages, collages, or assorted knick-knacks with a story behind them!
  25. Go for a drive around town. See if you can find any places you don't recognize, or any oddities while on the road.
  26. Plan your dream vacation. Why stop at one destination when the whole world awaits your imagination!
  27. Learn to play an instrument. Or try to learn a song on this virtual piano!
  28. Watch the sunset. Take the time to observe the sky changing colors, or the road lights turning on.
  29. Go for a hike. Many towns have nature hiking events that anyone can participate in. Just be sure to bring the right shoes and some water!
  30. Do some yoga. Decompress by learning new poses and finding your inner peace.
  31. Learn the benefits of meal-prepping. It can take a lot of time and thinking off your hands.
  32. Learn calligraphy, or improve your handwriting. Calligraphy is an important skill, and can be used to make a fancy signature!
  33. Give yourself a makeover. And not just the makeup kind; try out some different clothes, or an accessory you never got to style.
  34. Start a blog. Starting a blog on a dedicated website is not as hard as it seems, and can leave you a little more tech savvy!
  35. Watch your favorite TV show. See how many episodes you can get in!
  36. Visit your local library. Many libraries have toys and DVDs you can borrow, not just books.
  37. Visit a stand-up comedy or karaoke event. Local bars and restaurants can host open-mic nights, so try singing your favorite song and building your confidence.
  38. Go thrift shopping, or shop small-business. Shopping secondhand can lessen your carbon footprint, while shopping from small businesses supports passionate people at work!
  39. Volunteer, and try to help out at an animal shelter or soup kitchen.
  40. Go for a bike ride around your neighborhood, and feel the breeze on your face.
  41. Buy a yo-yo and learn how to walk the dog or rock the baby.
  42. Take a bubble bath. Pick your favorite bath bomb, and soak in the suds!
  43. Make a gratitude list. Write down what you're grateful for, even if it's something small!
  44. Get into arts and crafts, and learn how to fold a paper crane or make a pop-up card.
  45. Take an online class in a topic you're interested in. It doesn't have to be a formal traditional class, an online YouTube series will do.
  46. Go to your local park, and take in your surroundings.
  47. Visit a farmer's market, and pick out some healthy, in-season produce.
  48. Start a YouTube channel. Make it about anything, or teach the world about a skill you've mastered.
  49. Grab some paint and follow a Bob Ross painting episode.
  50. Watch a sports game. Take the time to learn about the sport, and how points are scored.

Affirmations

Feeling sad is just another human emotion, yet sometimes it can feel like this feeling won't go away any time soon. In even worse cases, we can feel as if sadness controls us. By reciting simple affirmations, it can shift your mindset to think more positively and be more hopeful of the situation.

Even if you're not sad, try repeating these affirmations to yourself, and checking in on how you feel.

(Taken from Power of Positivity's sadness affirmations)

  • I am strong and resilient.
  • I am more than my sadness, and I don't let it define me.
  • I am taking things one step at a time.
  • I am hopeful about the future.
  • I am worthy of love and happiness.
  • Everything is okay right now.
  • I always find a way to pick myself back up.
